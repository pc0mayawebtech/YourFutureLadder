Keypoints for Health And Care Visa

The Health and Care Visa is a specific type of visa offered by the UK government to healthcare professionals who wish to work in the UK's National Health Service (NHS), social care sector, or for eligible NHS organizations. Here are the key points regarding the Health and Care Visa:

Eligibility Criteria

Healthcare professionals such as doctors, nurses, paramedics, and other healthcare workers, including social workers, who have a job offer from the NHS, an NHS supplier, or a social care provider that is registered with the Care Quality Commission (CQC) can apply for this visa.

Sponsorship Requirement

Applicants must have a job offer from a UK employer that holds a sponsor license issued by the UK Home Office. The employer must issue a Certificate of Sponsorship (COS) to the applicant before they can apply for the visa.

Fast-track Process

The Health and Care Visa offers a fast-track immigration process, enabling healthcare professionals to start work in the UK more quickly. This includes reduced application fees and priority processing.

English Language Requirement

Applicants must demonstrate their proficiency in English by passing an approved English language test or by having an academic qualification that was taught or researched in English.
